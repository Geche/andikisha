KRA revised the PAYE tax brackets effective January 2026 as part of the annual Finance Act updates. For most employers, this means recalculating monthly tax deductions for any employee earning above KES 24,000.

## What exactly changed

The key changes affect the third and fourth income bands. The full revised structure is:

- **Band 1:** KES 0 – 24,000 at **10%**
- **Band 2:** KES 24,001 – 32,333 at **25%**
- **Band 3:** KES 32,334 – 500,000 at **30%** *(revised)*
- **Band 4:** KES 500,001 – 800,000 at **32.5%**
- **Band 5:** Above KES 800,000 at **35%**

Personal relief also increased from KES 2,400 to **KES 3,200 per month**. Insurance relief remains at 15% of NHIF/SHIF contributions, capped at KES 5,000 per month.

## Who is affected

Any employee earning above KES 32,333 per month is affected. For a company with 30 employees where the average salary sits at KES 60,000, every single payroll line for those employees needs recalculating.

The change is straightforward in principle. The difficulty is in execution — particularly if you are running payroll on spreadsheets where tax tables are hardcoded formulas.

## The practical risk for employers

A missed bracket update causes **under-deduction**. The employee technically owes KRA the difference. The employer, having remitted the wrong amount, faces a late remittance risk on the shortfall. KRA's penalty for incorrect PAYE calculation is 5% of the underpaid amount plus interest at 1% per month.

If you process January payroll at the old rates and do not catch it until the April reconciliation, you have three months of compounding underpayment to address.

## What to do if you are not on an automated system

Check your tax table now. Do not wait until the April reconciliation.

If you are using Excel, find the cell or named range where your PAYE brackets are defined. Update Band 3 to the new rate and confirm your personal relief cell reflects KES 3,200. Run a test calculation against a mid-range salary to verify the output.

If your payroll provider has not communicated an update to you yet, ask them directly whether the January 2026 rates are in effect. If they cannot confirm it, that is a problem.
